# Amorfos — Tone of Voice

> Companion to [`brand.md`](./brand.md). This is the deep reference for **how Amorfos
> sounds**. If `brand.md` §3 is the summary, this is the manual. When in doubt,
> choose the calmer, more certified, less promise-y line.

---

## 1. Voice in one sentence

> **Premium, spiritual, distinctly Indian — but restrained. Calm and knowledgeable,
> never hypey.** A house that honours a sacred tradition and backs every word with
> certification.

Reference brands for *feel*: **Nicobar** and **Forest Essentials** — Indian, elevated,
understated, confident without shouting. We are the opposite of a marketplace
listing or a loud bazaar stall.

---

## 2. Personality traits (with what they sound like)

| Trait | We are… | We are NOT… |
|---|---|---|
| **Reverent** | respectful of tradition, unhurried, sincere | preachy, sanctimonious, "holier-than-thou" |
| **Certain** | quietly confident, evidence-led (certification) | boastful, superlative-stacking, defensive |
| **Warm & human** | personal, approachable, "a message away" | corporate, robotic, faceless |
| **Quietly premium** | spare, elegant, considered, lets one good word land | ornate, purple-prose, cluttered |
| **Honest** | plain about what we sell and don't promise | hype-y, miracle-selling, vague |

**Litmus test:** *Could a thoughtful pandit and a design-led founder both nod at
this sentence?* If yes, it's on-brand.

## 3. Style mechanics

- **Sentence length:** mostly short. Vary rhythm; let a 3-word sentence breathe.
- **Punctuation:** the em-dash is our signature pause — used sparingly. Avoid
  exclamation marks almost entirely (≤ 0 in most copy; a rare, gentle one max).
- **Capitalisation:** sentence case everywhere except the eyebrow style (small,
  UPPERCASE, wide-tracked, gold). Never ALL-CAPS for emphasis in body copy.
- **Numbers:** "108 + 1 beads", "5 Mukhi", "7-day returns", "₹999". Prices in ₹.
- **Spelling:** **Indian English** — favour, colour, jeweller, energise, honour.
- **Sanskrit/Hindi terms:** use naturally and correctly — *mukhi, mala, japa, puja,
  dosh, guru bead, Rudraksha*. Capitalise *Rudraksha* and *Mukhi* as the brand does.
  Gloss lightly on first use in long-form ("its faces — its *mukhi*").
- **Italics:** for botanical (*Elaeocarpus ganitrus*), Sanskrit terms on first use,
  and the occasional emphasised word ("*steadies* you").
- **Emoji:** rare and tasteful (🪔 🤎 🕉). Never strings, never 🔥/💰/‼️.

## 4. The tradition-framing formula (use everywhere benefits appear)

Never state a benefit as a fact or outcome. Use this structure:

> **[Mukhi/bead] is traditionally associated with [quality] and [deity/planet],
> and is worn on the recommendation of astrologers and pandits.**

Softeners that keep us safe and on-brand: *traditionally · said to · associated
with · in tradition · on the recommendation of.*

- ✅ "The 7 Mukhi is traditionally associated with prosperity and Goddess
  Mahalakshmi, and is said to ease the effects of Shani."
- 🚫 "The 7 Mukhi brings you wealth and removes your money problems."

## 5. Dos & don'ts (with examples)

### DO
- **Lead with the bead and its certification.**
  ✅ *"A hand-selected Nepal bead, Lab Certified, set in silver on red thread."*
- **Educate, then invite.**
  ✅ *"Each face — each mukhi — carries a ruling deity and planet. Find the one for you."*
- **Be specific and sensory.**
  ✅ *"A teardrop, turned to stone."* · *"calm, clarity, a quieter mind."*
- **Sound like a person.**
  ✅ *"Questions about which mukhi to choose? Message us — we usually reply the same day."*
- **Let white space and restraint carry the premium feel.**
  ✅ *"Not a bazaar. A house."*

### DON'T
- 🚫 **Hype or urgency.** *"BUY NOW! Limited stock! 🔥🔥🔥"*
- 🚫 **Miracle/medical claims.** *"Cures anxiety, guarantees success."*
- 🚫 **Name a lab.** *"Certified by [X Institute]."* → just *"Lab Certified."*
- 🚫 **Bazaar/discount voice.** *"Cheapest rudraksha, lowest price guaranteed!"*
- 🚫 **Over-explain or over-decorate.** Long, flowery, adjective-stuffed sentences.
- 🚫 **Disparage rivals.** *"Unlike the fakes everyone else sells…"*

## 7. Channel voice cheats (length/format change, voice doesn't)

- **Homepage / hero:** evocative + short. *"The bead that steadies you."*
- **Product copy:** one-line tagline + tradition-framed description + 3 benefits +
  certification line. Calm and factual.
- **Email subject:** specific, ≤ ~55 chars, ≤ 1 emoji, no clickbait.
- **Instagram caption:** one evocative opener → one useful fact → soft CTA →
  ≤ 8 tasteful hashtags.
- **Ad headline:** the differentiator, plainly. *"Lab Certified Rudraksha, sent sealed."*
- **Journal:** teacherly, structured, scannable; tradition-framed; links to the
  matching collection.
- **Support/WhatsApp:** warm, prompt, helpful; like a knowledgeable friend at the house.

## 8. Words & phrases

**House words (use freely):** certified, Lab Certified, hand-selected, sealed,
authentic, hand-finished silver, red thread, mukhi, mala, japa, tradition,
recommendation of astrologers and pandits, considered, a house, steadies, calm,
clarity, resolve, provenance.

**Signature lines (reuse):** "Not a bazaar. A house." · "Authenticity is not a
claim — it's documented." · "We share tradition, never miracles." ·
"Certified. Sealed. Sent with care." · "The bead that steadies you."

**Banned words (never):** miracle, magic(al), cure, heal, treat, guaranteed,
instant, scientifically proven, 100%, cheapest, #1 (unprovable), [any lab name],
"removes all", "changes your life". (Full guardrails: `brand.md` §8.)
