## 1. Brand identity

### Name
**Amorfos.** Used as "Amorfos" in prose; product titles carry the registered mark
"**Amorfos®**" (e.g. *Amorfos® Lab Certified 7 Mukhi Rudraksha Pendant*). Never
abbreviate, never lowercase mid-sentence as a logo style ("amorfos" only appears
in the domain/handle).

### Mission
To make a sacred bead **trustworthy** — sourced honestly, Lab Certified properly,
and presented with the care of a fine object, so anyone in India can wear a
Rudraksha knowing exactly what it is.

### Vision
To be India's most trusted house for authentic Rudraksha — the calm, certified
alternative to a marketplace listing or a crowded bazaar stall.

### Positioning statement
> For devout and discerning Indians who want a genuine Rudraksha they can trust,
> **Amorfos** is a Delhi house that sells only **Lab Certified** beads —
> hand-selected from Nepal and Indonesia, finished in silver, and sent sealed with
> their certificate. Unlike marketplace sellers and unverified bazaar stalls, we
> treat each bead as a fine object and back it with documented authenticity and a
> real person to talk to.

### Unique value proposition (the proof, not the promise)
1. **Lab Certified, always.** Every bead is certified for authenticity and origin
   *before* it ships; the certificate travels with the piece. "Authenticity is not
   a claim — it's documented."
2. **Hand-selected at source.** Beads chosen individually from Nepal and Indonesia
   for face, form and finish; the better pieces set in hand-finished silver.
3. **A house, not a bazaar.** Considered, premium presentation — calm pages, honest
   counsel, sealed delivery.
4. **A real person on the other end.** WhatsApp the brand directly; usually a
   same-day reply. Help choosing a mukhi is part of the offer.
5. **Earned credibility.** Thousands of beads previously sold on Amazon and
   Flipkart — the same care, now direct.

### What we sell (so copy stays accurate)
- **Forms:** Pendants (single bead in silver on red thread), Malas (108 + 1 beads,
  hand-knotted, for japa), Combinations (multiple mukhi linked in silver for
  traditional remedial sets, e.g. the Kaal Sarp Dosh set of 8+9+10 Mukhi), and
  Loose beads (unstrung, single large-format mukhi).
- **Faces (mukhi):** 1 through 14, plus Gauri Shankar and Ganesh. Each mukhi has a
  traditional ruling **deity** and **planet** (e.g. 5 Mukhi → Kalagni Rudra /
  Jupiter; 7 Mukhi → Mahalakshmi / Saturn). State these as *tradition*, never as
  guaranteed effect.
- **Origin:** Nepal and Indonesia (a genuine, neutral quality distinction — not
  "good vs bad").
- **Materials:** Hand-selected Rudraksha bead · hand-finished silver capping · red
  thread. The botanical source is *Elaeocarpus ganitrus*.

## 2. Target audience

### Primary persona — "The Devout Researcher"
- **Who:** Indian adults, ~28–55, practising or culturally Hindu, buying for
  themselves or a family member. Tier-1/2 cities and the diaspora; comfortable
  buying online but cautious with spiritual purchases.
- **Pain points:**
  - *"Is this even real?"* — fear of fakes, dyed seeds, glued faces, relabelled beads.
  - Overwhelmed by mukhi/origin/price variation and conflicting blog advice.
  - Marketplace listings feel transactional and untrustworthy for something sacred.
  - Doesn't want to be sold a "miracle"; wants the genuine article and honest guidance.
- **Motivations:** Devotion, peace of mind, a steadying daily ritual, acting on an
  astrologer's/pandit's recommendation, gifting something meaningful.
- **What converts them:** Visible certification, plain-spoken authenticity, the
  ability to ask a real person, restrained premium presentation.

### Secondary personas
- **The Remedy-Seeker (astrologer-directed).** Has been told to wear a specific
  mukhi or combination (often Kaal Sarp Dosh, or a study/career set). Needs the
  *right, certified* bead quickly, with confidence it matches the recommendation.
  Copy角度: certified, correct mukhi, sent sealed — *we share tradition, the
  astrologer advises the remedy.*
- **The Gifter.** Buying for a parent, spouse or for a milestone (housewarming,
  exam season, new venture). Cares about presentation, certificate, and that it
  "feels chosen." Copy angle: a fine object, sealed and certified, chosen for them.
- **The Collector / Self-stringer.** Wants loose, large-format, rarer mukhi to set
  or keep in puja. Copy angle: provenance, face/form/finish, certification of each bead.

> **Always write to the *Devout Researcher* by default.** The others are variations
> on the same person at a different moment.

## 3. Tone & voice (summary — full guide in `tone-of-voice.md`)

**Voice in one line:** *Premium, spiritual, distinctly Indian — but restrained.
Calm and knowledgeable, never hypey.* (Think Nicobar / Forest Essentials, not a
marketplace listing.)

**Five personality traits**
1. **Reverent, not preachy** — we honour the tradition without sermonising.
2. **Certain, not boastful** — confidence comes from certification, not adjectives.
3. **Warm & human** — a house you can message, not a faceless store.
4. **Quietly premium** — elegant, spare, considered; lets white space and one
   good word do the work.
5. **Honest** — "we share tradition, never miracles."

**Dos**
- Lead with the bead and its certification.
- Use tradition-framed language: *"traditionally associated with…"*, *"worn on the
  recommendation of astrologers and pandits."*
- Short, calm sentences. Concrete nouns (bead, mukhi, silver, certificate, thread).
- Indian English and Sanskrit terms used naturally and correctly (mukhi, japa,
  mala, puja, dosh, guru bead) — with a light gloss when needed.

**Don'ts**
- 🚫 No hype, urgency, or shouting ("BUY NOW!!!", "🔥🔥", "LAST CHANCE").
- 🚫 No medical or miraculous claims (see §8).
- 🚫 No naming a certifying lab.
- 🚫 No bazaar/discount-clearance tone ("cheapest", "lowest price guaranteed").
- 🚫 No disparaging competitors by name.

**Tone snapshot**
- ✅ *"The everyday bead — calm, clarity, a quieter mind."*
- ✅ *"Authenticity is not a claim — it's documented."*
- 🚫 *"This miracle bead will change your life instantly — guaranteed!"*
- 🚫 *"India's #1 rudraksha at unbeatable prices, hurry!"*

> **The brand is light, warm and airy.** A dark theme was tried early and
> **rejected as "too dark."** Do **not** design dark-mode marketing. The only dark
> surfaces allowed are small chips/gradients that sit *over product photography*
> (dark ground, cream `#f6f1e7` text).

### Typography
- **Cormorant Garamond** — headings, display, anything elegant (serif). Weight ~500,
  tight leading. Used for the "voice" of the brand.
- **Inter** — body, UI, captions (sans). Highly legible.
- **Eyebrow style** — small, UPPERCASE, wide letter-spacing (~0.32em), gold. Used
  above headings (e.g. "OUR STORY", "THE SEED OF SHIVA").

### Logo
- Dark, calligraphic wordmark used **directly on the light ivory page** — no CSS
  inversion, no dark box behind it. Give it generous clear space. Never stretch,
  recolour, add effects, or place on a busy photo without a clean field.

### Imagery
- **Product shots:** full product, **centred, on white, fit-to-image** (Amazon-style,
  never cropped into rectangles). Show the certificate where relevant.
- **Editorial:** warm, natural light; soft shadows; calm hands; uncluttered. Ivory,
  brown, silver, a touch of red thread. Subtle film grain is on-brand.
- Avoid: neon, harsh saturation, busy collages, stocky "spiritual" clichés (glowing
  auras, lens flares), cluttered bazaar scenes.

## 6. Channel guidelines

The voice never changes; **format and length** adapt. Default to less.

### Email
- **From:** Amorfos · `care@amorfos.in`. Warm, personal, signed like a house.
- **Subject lines:** calm and specific, not clickbait. ≤ ~55 chars, ≤ 1 emoji
  (often none).
  - ✅ *"Your 7 Mukhi, certified and on its way"* · *"How we certify every bead"*
  - 🚫 *"🔥 50% OFF — don't miss this!!!"*
- **Body:** one idea per email, short paragraphs, one clear CTA. Lead with substance
  (certification, a guide, a new bead), not a discount.
- **Always include:** a real way to reach a person (WhatsApp/email).
- Transactional emails (order, shipping) stay reassuring and precise.

### Social (Instagram / Facebook / WhatsApp broadcast)
- **Instagram:** image/reel-led. Caption opens with one calm, evocative line, then
  one useful fact (mukhi/origin/certification), then a soft CTA. 3–8 tasteful
  hashtags max, lowercase, relevant (#rudraksha #labcertified #mukhi). No hashtag walls.
- **Tone of captions:** like the homepage — *"The bead that steadies you."* Educate
  (mukhi meaning, how we certify), don't hard-sell.
- **WhatsApp broadcast:** short, respectful, opt-in only. A line + a link. Never
  spammy, never daily.
- **Emoji:** sparing and tasteful (🪔 🤎 🕉 occasionally); never strings of fire/money emojis.

### Ads (Meta / Google)
- **Lead with the differentiator:** *Lab Certified · hand-selected · sent sealed.*
  Trust is the hook, not price.
- **Headlines:** clear and honest. ✅ *"Lab Certified Rudraksha, sent sealed."*
  🚫 *"Miracle Rudraksha — instant results!"* (also ad-policy-risky).
- **Google search copy:** match query intent (e.g. "5 mukhi rudraksha"), state
  certified + origin + free shipping above ₹999. No superlatives we can't prove.
- **Promotions:** allowed but understated — frame as value, never desperation. No
  fake countdowns or fake scarcity. (Genuine one-of-a-kind stock *may* be noted
  honestly.)

### Long-form (Journal / blog / landing pages)
- Governed by [`content-marketing-strategy.md`](./content-marketing-strategy.md).
  SEO-first, informational/commercial-investigation intent, funnels to
  `/collections/*` and `/shop/*`.
- Structure: clear H1/H2s, scannable, FAQ blocks, internal links to the matching
  collection. Answer the *question*, then link to the *product* — never cannibalise
  a collection page.
- Mukhi guides cover ruling deity, planet, who traditionally wears it (per
  astrologer/pandit), origin notes — all as **tradition**, no claims.
- Every draft passes the §8 banned-phrase check **and** human review before publishing.

### Packaging / unboxing copy
- Quiet and reassuring: the certificate, the seal, a thank-you, the WhatsApp line.
  "Certified. Sealed. Sent with care."

## 7. Competitive context

The Rudraksha market splits into **established authority sites**, **marketplaces**,
and **unverified bazaar/local sellers**. Position Amorfos as the *trustworthy,
premium, personal* option.

| Alternative | What they are | Their strength | Where Amorfos wins |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rudra Centre** (rudraksha-ratna.com) | Category giant, ~500K visits/mo, huge catalogue + content | Authority, selection, SEO dominance on head terms | We're calmer, more premium and personal; certified + sealed + a real person on WhatsApp; we win on long-tail/per-mukhi and trust, not on breadth |
| **Rudralife** | Established branded Rudraksha seller | Brand recognition, consultations | Restrained premium aesthetic, documented certification, direct/honest counsel without upsell pressure |
| **Marketplaces** (Amazon/Flipkart listings) | Where most buyers start | Convenience, reviews, Prime | Sealed + certified + curated presentation; a house, not a listing; *we came from there and brought the care direct* |
| **Local/bazaar & unverified online sellers** | Cheapest, ubiquitous | Price, immediacy | Authenticity is documented, not promised; this is our sharpest contrast |

**Rules of engagement**
- Compete on **trust, certification, curation and care** — not on price.
- **Do not name or disparage** competitors in public marketing. Differentiate by
  stating what *we* do (certified, sealed, hand-selected), not by attacking others.
- Acknowledge we are a newer, smaller house — and make that a virtue (every bead
  gets attention; a real person answers).
- On SEO: we win **per-mukhi, question, and "by rashi" long-tail**, not head terms
  (see content strategy). Don't claim category leadership we haven't earned.

## 8. What we never say (hard guardrails)

These are **non-negotiable** — legal, ethical and brand-defining. They are enforced
in the content pipeline by a banned-phrase filter (`lib/articleGenerator.ts`) and
by human review. Apply them to **every** channel.

### Never name a certifying lab
- ✅ "Lab Certified" · "certified for authenticity and origin"
- 🚫 Any specific lab name or institute. Just "Lab Certified."

### No medical claims
- 🚫 cures, heals, treats, remedies an illness, "lowers blood pressure",
  "fixes anxiety/depression", any health outcome, dosage, or therapeutic promise.
- ✅ Reframe to tradition: *"traditionally associated with calm and clarity of mind."*

### No miraculous / magical / guaranteed claims
- 🚫 miracle, magic, magical, instant results, guaranteed, "will make you rich",
  "removes all problems/negativity", "scientifically proven", "100% effective",
  "changes your life", "wards off all evil".
- ✅ *"said to support steadiness and focus, traditionally worn on the
  recommendation of astrologers and pandits."*
- **Always attribute** benefits to tradition + astrologer/pandit recommendation;
  never assert them as fact or outcome.

### No hype / bazaar / pressure language
- 🚫 "cheapest", "lowest price guaranteed", "#1 in India" (unprovable), fake
  countdowns, fake "only 1 left!" unless genuinely true, "🔥 hurry!!!", ALL-CAPS shouting.

### No disparaging or comparative attacks
- 🚫 Naming competitors to put them down; "unlike [brand], we're not fake."

### Don't over-promise logistics or returns
- ✅ State the real policy: free shipping above ₹999, flat ₹79 below; 7-day returns
  on **unused, sealed** products; prepaid only (no COD currently).
- 🚫 "free returns on everything", "next-day delivery anywhere", "lifetime guarantee".

### Topics to avoid / handle with care
- Religion as exclusionary or political; caste; other faiths comparatively;
  pandemic/illness as a hook; astrology as deterministic fact.
- Keep spiritual content **inclusive, respectful and traditional in framing.**

### Quick rewrite table
| 🚫 Off-brand | ✅ On-brand |
|---|---|
| "This miracle bead cures stress and guarantees wealth." | "The 5 Mukhi is traditionally associated with calm and clarity, worn on the recommendation of astrologers and pandits." |
| "Certified by [Lab Name] — 100% genuine!" | "Lab Certified for authenticity and origin — the certificate ships with every bead." |
| "Cheapest rudraksha in India, hurry — sale ends tonight!" | "Hand-selected, certified Rudraksha. Free shipping above ₹999." |
| "Unlike fake sellers, ours actually works." | "Every Amorfos bead is Lab Certified and sealed before it ships — authenticity is documented, not claimed." |
