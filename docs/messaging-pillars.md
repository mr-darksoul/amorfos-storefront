# Amorfos — Messaging Pillars

> Companion to [`brand.md`](./brand.md). The **five core themes** every Amorfos
> marketing asset should reinforce. A strong asset **leads with one** pillar and
> **supports with a second**. Nothing we publish may contradict any pillar or the
> guardrails in `brand.md` §8.

---

## How to use these pillars

1. Pick the **lead pillar** that matches the asset's goal and audience moment.
2. Pick a **support pillar** for the secondary line / proof.
3. Pull a **proof point** (below) — don't invent new claims.
4. Run it past `brand.md` §10 checklist before shipping.

| Goal / moment | Lead pillar | Support pillar |
|---|---|---|
| Cold awareness, "is this real?" | 1 Certified authenticity | 5 Earned trust |
| Premium/gift positioning | 4 A house, not a bazaar | 2 Hand-selected |
| Educational / mukhi content | 3 Tradition, honestly told | 1 Certified authenticity |
| Conversion / cart nudge | 5 Earned trust | 1 Certified authenticity |
| Provenance / collector | 2 Hand-selected | 3 Tradition, honestly told |

## Pillar 1 — Certified authenticity
**Core idea:** The single biggest reason to choose Amorfos. Every bead is **Lab
Certified** for authenticity and origin, sealed, and ships with its certificate.
Proof over promise.

- **Say it like:** *"Authenticity is not a claim — it's documented."* ·
  *"Certified. Sealed. Sent with care."*
- **Proof points:** Lab Certified before shipping · certificate accompanies the
  piece · each bead in a combination certified individually · sealed for authenticity.
- **Guardrail:** "Lab Certified" only — **never name a lab** (`brand.md` §8).

## Pillar 2 — Hand-selected at source
**Core idea:** These aren't relabelled, middle-market beads. Each is chosen by hand
from **Nepal and Indonesia** for face, form and finish; the better pieces are set
in hand-finished silver.

- **Say it like:** *"Chosen bead by bead — the finer pieces set in silver."*
- **Proof points:** sourced from Nepal & Indonesia · selected for face/form/finish ·
  hand-finished silver capping on red thread · "no relabelled middle-market beads."
- **Use for:** provenance stories, origin guides (Nepal vs Indonesia), collector/loose beads.

## Pillar 3 — Tradition, honestly told
**Core idea:** We carry deep, genuine tradition — deity, planet, purpose for each
mukhi — but frame it as heritage, never as guaranteed outcome. **"We share
tradition, never miracles."** The astrologer or pandit advises; we supply the
certified bead.

- **Say it like:** *"Each face carries a ruling deity and planet, and a quiet purpose."*
- **Proof points:** mukhi → deity/planet associations · "worn on the recommendation
  of astrologers and pandits" · *Elaeocarpus ganitrus*, "the seed of Shiva", 108 + 1 mala.
- **Guardrail:** No medical/miraculous claims; always tradition-framed (`brand.md` §8,
  `tone-of-voice.md` §4).

## Pillar 4 — A house, not a bazaar
**Core idea:** A sacred bead deserves to be presented like a **fine object** —
calm, considered, premium — with a **real person** to talk to. The antidote to a
crowded listing or a bazaar stall.

- **Say it like:** *"Not a bazaar. A house."* · *"A quieter way to wear something ancient."*
- **Proof points:** restrained premium presentation · sealed delivery · WhatsApp a
  real person, usually same-day reply · honest counsel on choosing a mukhi.
- **Use for:** brand/awareness, gifting, premium positioning.

## Pillar 5 — Earned trust, now direct
**Core idea:** Amorfos didn't appear overnight. **Thousands of beads sold on Amazon
and Flipkart** taught us what people want — certainty. Going direct lets us do it
properly, backed by fair, plain policies.

- **Say it like:** *"The same care we gave thousands of customers — now direct to you."*
- **Proof points:** track record on Amazon/Flipkart · 7-day returns on unused, sealed ·
  free shipping above ₹999 · Delhi house, founded by Manav Bansal.
- **Use for:** conversion, trust-building, first-time-buyer reassurance.
